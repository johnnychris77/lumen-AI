# Global Quality Standard Framework

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Published
**Maintained by:** GSIN Standards Committee

---

## Purpose

This framework establishes unified quality classification standards for surgical instrument inspection across the Global Surgical Intelligence Network (GSIN). Standards are derived from anonymized aggregate network data, aligned with international regulatory requirements, and approved through the Advisory Consortium governance process.

**Important disclaimer:** All standards are evidence-informed guidance based on aggregate network observations. They do not constitute regulatory clearance or approval. Implementation requires local clinical and regulatory review. Human review required before any operational decisions.

---

## 1. Contamination Classification Standard (v2.1)

### Overview

A four-tier contamination severity classification applicable to all surgical instrument categories monitored within the GSIN network. Aligned with ISO 17664 and AAMI ST79.

### Tier Definitions

| Tier | Designation | Criteria | Required Action |
|------|-------------|----------|-----------------|
| Tier 1 | Critical | Visible biological contamination or confirmed pathogen presence | Immediate removal from service; incident report; CAPA |
| Tier 2 | Major | Residual protein >200 mcg/device or biofilm detected by ATP | Remove from service; root cause investigation; regulatory notification if applicable |
| Tier 3 | Moderate | Residual protein 50–200 mcg/device or surface pitting | Reprocessing review; technician investigation |
| Tier 4 | Minor | Cosmetic staining without functional impact | Document; monitor at next inspection cycle |

### Instrument Categories

Applies to: flexible scopes, rigid scopes, laparoscopic instruments, powered instruments, retractors, orthopaedic instruments, cardiovascular instruments.

### Regulatory Alignment

| Framework | Reference |
|-----------|-----------|
| ISO | 17664 |
| AAMI | ST79 |
| EU MDR | Annex I — General Safety and Performance Requirements |
| FDA | 21 CFR Part 820 (QMSR) |

## 2. Instrument Defect Classification Standard (v1.4)

### Overview

Standardized grading for physical integrity failures. Enables cross-network defect trend analysis and early warning detection.

### Grade Definitions

| Grade | Designation | Criteria |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Grade A | Critical | Functional compromise — instrument must be removed from service immediately |
| Grade B | Major | Significant defect requiring immediate inspection, repair evaluation, and quality review |
| Grade C | Moderate | Defect requiring monitoring and scheduled maintenance intervention |
| Grade D | Minor | Cosmetic defect with no functional or patient safety impact |

### Defect Categories

- Pitting and corrosion
- Mechanical deformation
- Identification marking degradation (label, laser etch, UDI)
- Seal and joint integrity failures
- Surface coating failure

### Regulatory Alignment

| Framework | Reference |
|-----------|-----------|
| FDA | 21 CFR Part 820 |
| EU MDR | Article 10 — Obligations of manufacturers |
| TGA | ARTG listing requirements |
| ISO | 13485 Section 8.5 |

## 3. Baseline Variance Tolerance Standard (v1.0)

### Overview

Defines acceptable variance thresholds for facility-level instrument quality baselines against GSIN network-published reference baselines. Enables early detection of systematic deviations.

### Variance Zones

| Zone | Definition | Action |
|------|------------|--------|
| Within Tolerance | Facility rate within ±1.5 SD of network baseline | Continue standard monitoring |
| Watch Zone | Facility rate 1.5–2.5 SD from network baseline | Increase monitoring frequency; internal review |
| Alert Zone | Facility rate >2.5 SD from network baseline | Investigation recommended; CAPA consideration |
| Critical Deviation | Facility rate >3 SD | Immediate review required; escalation to quality leadership |

### Measurement Dimensions

- Contamination rate (by instrument category)
- Physical defect rate
- Inspection pass rate
- CAPA closure rate
- Cycle count compliance

### Regulatory Alignment

| Framework | Reference |
|-----------|-----------|
| ISO | 13485 — Quality Management Systems |
| FDA | QMSR (Quality Management System Regulation) |
| EU MDR | Regulation 2017/745 — Post-market surveillance |

## 4. Inspection Scoring Standard (v3.0)

### Overview

Unified 100-point inspection scoring rubric enabling cross-network benchmarking. Applicable to all instrument categories.

### Scoring Rubric

| Domain | Points | Components |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Visual Inspection | 30 | Physical integrity (15), instrument identification (10), surface condition (5) |
| Cleanliness Verification | 35 | ATP result (15), protein residue assay (12), biofilm indicators (8) |
| Functional Testing | 25 | Mechanism/articulation (12), seal integrity (8), operational performance (5) |
| Documentation | 10 | Traceability records (5), service history (3), cycle count compliance (2) |

### Score Interpretation

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 95–100 | Excellent | Release for use |
| 85–94 | Acceptable | Release with documented note |
| 70–84 | Marginal | Conditional release; monitoring required |
| <70 | Failing | Remove from service; investigation required |

### Regulatory Alignment

| Framework | Reference |
|-----------|-----------|
| AAMI | ST79 |
| ISO | 15883 — Washer-disinfectors |
| FDA | 21 CFR Part 820 |
| EU MDR | Regulation 2017/745 |

## Governance

### Standard Lifecycle

```
Proposal → Working Group Draft → Advisory Consortium Review →
Public Comment (30–60 days) → Steering Committee Vote → Publication → Versioning
```

### Versioning Policy

- Minor updates (x.Y): Editorial corrections, regulatory alignment updates — Steering Committee approval
- Major updates (X.0): Criteria changes, new categories — Full consortium review required
- Deprecated standards: Maintained in archive for 3 years

### Human Review Requirement

All standard applications to operational decisions require human review. No autonomous action should be taken based on standard classifications alone without clinical and quality professional review.
