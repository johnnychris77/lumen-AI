# 1. Executive Overview

LumenAI v1.3 prepares the platform for enterprise customer pilot evaluation.

LumenAI is positioned as a healthcare governance intelligence platform that helps leaders move from reactive reporting to predictive oversight by connecting:

- Sterile processing quality signals
- CAPA trend intelligence
- Vendor performance accountability
- Power BI-ready analytics
- Executive recommendations
- Next-action guidance
- Evidence-backed release governance

The v1.3 pilot readiness phase builds on the sealed v1.2 Predictive Governance Intelligence public launch.

---

# 2. Customer Problem

Healthcare leaders often manage sterile processing quality, CAPA activity, vendor issues, and executive reporting through disconnected systems, manual spreadsheets, delayed dashboards, and fragmented governance workflows.

Common challenges include:

- Limited visibility into recurring quality risks
- Delayed CAPA escalation
- Inconsistent vendor accountability
- Manual executive reporting
- Limited trend intelligence
- Difficulty translating operational issues into leadership-ready metrics
- Limited evidence trail for governance decisions
- Fragmented reporting across quality, operations, and vendor governance

LumenAI addresses these challenges by organizing governance signals into a structured intelligence layer.

---

# 3. LumenAI Pilot Value Proposition

The enterprise pilot is designed to validate whether LumenAI can help healthcare leaders:

- Identify quality risk earlier
- Detect CAPA recurrence and aging risk
- Monitor vendor performance trends
- Link vendor events to CAPA governance
- Export governance metrics for Power BI
- Support executive review and leadership watch workflows
- Reduce manual reporting burden
- Improve governance meeting readiness
- Create an evidence-backed decision trail

# 7. Pilot Data Boundary

The initial pilot is designed to support a non-PHI evaluation by default.

The pilot should use:

- Demo data
- Synthetic data
- De-identified examples
- Aggregated governance categories
- Non-PHI quality event categories
- Non-sensitive CAPA examples
- Non-sensitive vendor governance examples

The pilot should avoid:

- PHI
- Patient identifiers
- Employee-sensitive records
- Confidential customer records
- Live EHR feeds
- Live instrument tracking feeds
- Unapproved customer production data

If future phases require PHI, production data, or system integrations, those phases should undergo separate customer privacy, security, compliance, legal, and contracting review.

# 9. Pilot Success Criteria

The pilot should be considered successful if the customer confirms:

- LumenAI addresses a real governance visibility gap
- CAPA trend intelligence is valuable to leadership
- Vendor trend intelligence supports accountability
- Power BI export readiness supports reporting needs
- Dashboard cards are understandable and executive-ready
- CSV exports and data dictionary are useful
- Customer stakeholders understand the value proposition
- A next-step commercial or expanded pilot path is identified
