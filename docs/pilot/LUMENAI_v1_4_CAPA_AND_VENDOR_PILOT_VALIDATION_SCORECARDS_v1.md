# 3. Scoring Model

Each validation category should be scored from 1 to 5.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 5 | Strongly validated |
| 4 | Validated with minor changes |
| 3 | Partially validated |
| 2 | Weakly validated |
| 1 | Not validated |

Recommended interpretation:

| Average Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 4.5 to 5.0 | Strongly validated for commercial or expanded pilot |
| 3.8 to 4.4 | Validated with refinement |
| 3.0 to 3.7 | Continue discovery and workflow refinement |
| 2.0 to 2.9 | Significant validation gaps remain |
| Below 2.0 | Use case not validated |

# 9. Decision Thresholds

## Strongly Validated

Criteria:
- Average score 4.5 or higher
- Executive escalation logic score 4 or higher
- Governance meeting usefulness score 4 or higher
- Commercial readiness score 4 or higher

Recommended action:
Include CAPA/vendor governance in commercial proposal or expanded pilot.

---

## Validated With Refinement

Criteria:
- Average score 3.8 to 4.4
- Customer validates core value
- Refinements are manageable

Recommended action:
Refine thresholds, fields, and workflow before final executive review.

---

## Continue Discovery

Criteria:
- Average score 3.0 to 3.7
- Customer sees value but needs more workflow mapping or examples

Recommended action:
Continue use case discovery and repeat validation.

---

## Significant Validation Gaps

Criteria:
- Average score 2.0 to 2.9

Recommended action:
Reassess CAPA/vendor scope before commercial discussion.

---

## Not Validated

Criteria:
- Average score below 2.0

Recommended action:
Do not position the use case as a validated commercial driver.
