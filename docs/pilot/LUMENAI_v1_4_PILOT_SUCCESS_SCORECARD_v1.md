## Final Determination
The LumenAI v1.4 Pilot Success Scorecard v1 is drafted.

This scorecard defines how LumenAI will measure pilot success across customer problem fit, workflow fit, stakeholder engagement, data boundary fit, Power BI validation, CAPA validation, vendor governance validation, executive value, ROI value, and commercial conversion readiness.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of the pilot success scorecard is to create a structured method for evaluating whether a LumenAI enterprise pilot is successful.

The scorecard supports:

- Pilot progress tracking
- Executive review preparation
- Customer value validation
- Evidence-backed pilot closeout
- ROI discussion
- Commercial conversion planning
- Expansion readiness evaluation

---

# 2. Scoring Model

Each category should be scored using a 1 to 5 scale.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 5 | Strongly validated |
| 4 | Validated with minor gaps |
| 3 | Partially validated |
| 2 | Weakly validated |
| 1 | Not validated |

Recommended interpretation:

| Average Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 4.5 to 5.0 | Strong commercial conversion candidate |
| 3.8 to 4.4 | Strong expanded pilot candidate |
| 3.0 to 3.7 | Continue discovery or refine pilot scope |
| 2.0 to 2.9 | Weak fit or unresolved barriers |
| Below 2.0 | Not ready or not a fit |

# 3. Scorecard Categories

## Category 1 — Customer Problem Fit

Question:
Does the customer confirm that LumenAI addresses a meaningful governance visibility problem?

Evidence sources:
- Discovery notes
- Stakeholder feedback
- Current-state workflow mapping
- Executive sponsor comments

Scoring guidance:
- 5: Customer clearly confirms major governance visibility gap
- 4: Customer confirms meaningful gap with minor uncertainty
- 3: Customer sees possible value but needs more validation
- 2: Customer problem is unclear or low-priority
- 1: Customer does not validate the problem

---

## Category 2 — Workflow Fit

Question:
Does LumenAI fit into the customer’s quality, CAPA, vendor, and executive reporting workflows?

Evidence sources:
- Current-state workflow map
- Future-state pilot workflow map
- Operational stakeholder feedback
- Use case validation notes

Scoring guidance:
- 5: Strong workflow fit across multiple governance workflows
- 4: Good fit with minor workflow adjustments needed
- 3: Partial fit with several workflow questions
- 2: Weak fit or significant workflow barriers
- 1: No practical workflow fit identified

---

## Category 3 — Stakeholder Engagement

Question:
Are the right customer stakeholders engaged and participating?

Evidence sources:
- Attendance records
- Weekly status reports
- Governance review notes
- Customer action follow-up

Scoring guidance:
- 5: Executive, operational, quality, vendor, analytics, and security stakeholders engaged
- 4: Most key stakeholders engaged
- 3: Some stakeholders engaged but gaps remain
- 2: Limited stakeholder participation
- 1: Stakeholder engagement insufficient

---

## Category 4 — Data Boundary Fit

Question:
Can the pilot operate within an acceptable data boundary, preferably non-PHI?

Evidence sources:
- Data boundary confirmation
- Security/privacy notes
- Customer approval notes
- Demo environment validation

Scoring guidance:
- 5: Non-PHI pilot boundary clearly accepted
- 4: Boundary accepted with minor follow-up
- 3: Boundary mostly accepted but review needed
- 2: Data boundary concerns remain unresolved
- 1: Data boundary blocks pilot execution

---

## Category 5 — Power BI Validation

Question:
Does the customer validate the Power BI export structure, data dictionary, and dashboard usefulness?

Evidence sources:
- Power BI validation notes
- Analytics/BI feedback
- Data dictionary review
- Export field review

Scoring guidance:
- 5: Analytics team strongly validates export and dashboard model
- 4: Validated with minor field changes
- 3: Partially validated with notable gaps
- 2: Significant analytics gaps remain
- 1: Power BI model not validated

---

## Category 6 — CAPA Governance Validation

Question:
Does the customer validate CAPA trend intelligence, recurrence visibility, aging risk, and escalation logic?

Evidence sources:
- CAPA validation notes
- Quality/CAPA stakeholder feedback
- Governance review notes
- Success criteria updates

Scoring guidance:
- 5: CAPA governance use case strongly validated
- 4: Validated with minor logic or field changes
- 3: Partially validated with refinement needed
- 2: Weak CAPA value confirmation
- 1: CAPA use case not validated

---

## Category 7 — Vendor Governance Validation

Question:
Does the customer validate vendor trend intelligence, repeat event visibility, CAPA linkage, and vendor escalation logic?

Evidence sources:
- Vendor governance validation notes
- Supply chain/vendor governance feedback
- Governance review notes
- Vendor use case feedback

Scoring guidance:
- 5: Vendor governance use case strongly validated
- 4: Validated with minor field or workflow changes
- 3: Partially validated with refinement needed
- 2: Weak vendor value confirmation
- 1: Vendor use case not validated

---

## Category 8 — Executive Value

Question:
Does the executive sponsor see value in using LumenAI for governance review and decision support?

Evidence sources:
- Executive sponsor feedback
- Governance review notes
- Executive review package
- Pilot outcome summary

Scoring guidance:
- 5: Executive sponsor strongly validates value
- 4: Executive value validated with minor questions
- 3: Executive value partially validated
- 2: Executive value unclear
- 1: Executive value not validated

---

## Category 9 — ROI and Operational Value

Question:
Does the pilot produce measurable or credible value signals?

Evidence sources:
- ROI/value framework
- Reporting burden estimates
- Stakeholder feedback
- Evidence capture package
- Pilot value scorecard

Scoring guidance:
- 5: Strong value signals and measurable ROI opportunity
- 4: Clear value signals with some estimates pending
- 3: Partial value signals
- 2: Weak or unclear value signals
- 1: No meaningful value signal

---

## Category 10 — Commercial Conversion Readiness

Question:
Is there a clear next-step pathway after the pilot?

Evidence sources:
- Executive review
- Commercial conversion recommendation
- Expansion readiness checklist
- Customer decision notes

Scoring guidance:
- 5: Commercial or expanded pilot path clearly identified
- 4: Strong next-step interest with minor barriers
- 3: Possible next step but more discovery required
- 2: Weak conversion pathway
- 1: No conversion path identified

# 6. Decision Thresholds

## Strong Commercial Conversion Candidate

Criteria:
- Average score 4.5 or higher
- Executive value score 4 or higher
- Commercial conversion readiness score 4 or higher
- No unresolved high-risk data boundary barrier

Recommended action:
Move to commercial proposal or paid pilot expansion.

---

## Strong Expanded Pilot Candidate

Criteria:
- Average score 3.8 to 4.4
- At least two use cases validated
- Customer wants more evaluation
- Barriers are manageable

Recommended action:
Propose expanded pilot or department-level pilot.

---

## Continue Discovery or Refine Pilot Scope

Criteria:
- Average score 3.0 to 3.7
- Customer sees value but requires more workflow, security, or analytics validation

Recommended action:
Continue discovery and refine pilot scope.

---

## Weak Fit or Unresolved Barriers

Criteria:
- Average score 2.0 to 2.9
- Value is unclear or barriers remain significant

Recommended action:
Pause or redesign pilot scope.

---

## Not Ready or Not a Fit

Criteria:
- Average score below 2.0
- Customer does not validate problem, value, or workflow fit

Recommended action:
Close as not ready or not a fit.
