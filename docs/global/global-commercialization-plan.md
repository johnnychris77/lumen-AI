## 1. Executive Summary

LumenAI's global commercialization strategy employs a tiered go-to-market model: direct enterprise sales in Tier 1 markets (Canada, UK, Australia, Singapore) where language and market familiarity support early direct engagement, and strategic distributor/reseller partnerships in Tier 2 and Tier 3 markets where local regulatory, language, and relationship requirements necessitate in-country commercial presence.

**Revenue Targets (Planning Estimates):**
- Year 1 International: CAD/USD equivalent of USD 2–4M ARR (Canada, Australia, Singapore)
- Year 2 International: USD 8–15M ARR (add UK, EU, Korea)
- Year 3 International: USD 20–35M ARR (scale all Tier 1–2; Japan pipeline)

## 2. Channel Strategy by Market Tier

### 2.1 Tier 1 Markets — Direct Enterprise Sales

**Markets:** Canada, United Kingdom, Australia, Singapore

**Model:** Direct sales with regional Account Executive (AE) coverage; supported by pre-sales solutions consultant (with SPD/clinical domain expertise)

**Rationale:**
- English-language primary markets (reduces communication friction)
- Market size justifies direct investment
- LumenAI brand must be established directly before distributor model
- Enterprise hospital procurement responds to direct vendor engagement
- Regulatory submission process benefits from direct manufacturer relationship

**Direct Sales Staffing (Year 1):**
- Canada: 1 x Enterprise AE (Toronto base), 1 x Solutions Consultant (SPD background)
- Australia: 1 x Enterprise AE (Sydney or Melbourne base), 1 x Solutions Consultant
- UK: 1 x Enterprise AE (London base), 1 x NHS Framework/Procurement Specialist
- Singapore: APAC Regional AE (Singapore base) — covers Singapore + regional ASEAN business development

**Sales Motion:**
1. Inbound/outbound prospecting through SPD association channels, conference presence
2. Executive briefing (CIO, CNO, SPD Director) — clinical and operational value demonstration
3. Pilot program (3–6 months; typically 1 SPD department; performance benchmarked)
4. Enterprise contract negotiation (multi-site, multi-year preferred)
5. Implementation and onboarding (regional CS)
6. Expansion (additional sites, modules)

### 2.2 Tier 2 Markets — Strategic Distributor Model

**Markets:** Germany (EU lead), South Korea, New Zealand (follow-on post-AU)

**Model:** Exclusive or preferred distribution partnership with qualified healthcare IT distributor/reseller; LumenAI provides product, training, regulatory support; distributor provides local market access, sales execution, and first-line support

**Distributor Criteria (Section 5):** See detailed criteria below

**LumenAI Role in Tier 2:**
- Master distribution agreement with revenue share model
- Co-selling support from LumenAI international sales team
- Joint marketing fund (JMF) for local events and digital marketing
- Product certification and training for distributor sales/support staff
- Regulatory documentation package (localized) provided to distributor
- Escalation pathway to LumenAI product/engineering for complex issues

### 2.3 Tier 3 Markets — Business Development / Pipeline Building

**Markets:** Japan, UAE, Saudi Arabia

**Model:** Early-stage business development, regulatory pathway investment, and partner identification — no revenue commitment in planning horizon; market preparation only

**Activities:**
- Regulatory pathway engagement (PMDA pre-consultation for Japan; SFDA/MOHAP for KSA/UAE)
- Distributor/MAH identification (Japan: DMAH partner search)
- Conference/exhibition presence (Arab Health for UAE/KSA; JPMA for Japan)
- Letter of intent or MOU with anchor hospital partners (not binding revenue)
- Language localization (Japanese, Arabic) investment begins in Year 2

## 4. Reseller and Distribution Partner Criteria

### 4.1 Core Criteria (All Markets)

| Criterion | Requirement | Weighting |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Healthcare IT expertise | Minimum 5 years active in healthcare IT sales/implementation | Essential |
| Medical device/SaaS experience | Experience distributing SaaS-based medical device or clinical software | Essential |
| Regulatory knowledge | Demonstrated understanding of local medical device regulatory framework | Essential |
| Local support capability | In-country tier-1 support team (minimum 2 qualified support engineers) | Essential |
| Revenue capacity | Minimum USD 5M annual revenue; financial stability | Essential |
| Reference accounts | Minimum 3 active hospital clients with implementable references | Essential |
| Language capability | Native language support team for local market | Essential |
| SPD/sterilization domain | At least 1 senior employee with SPD/CSSD domain expertise | Preferred |
| Integration capability | Ability to support HL7 FHIR or existing integration connections | Preferred |
| Exclusivity | Market exclusivity preferred (3-year initial term with performance gates) | Negotiated |

### 4.2 Market-Specific Partner Criteria

**Germany:**
- DIN EN ISO 13485 understanding (QMS for medical devices)
- Existing relationships with DGSV, German hospital procurement offices
- German-language sales and support team
- Established relationships with Getinge T-DOC or equivalent SPD system vendors
- Examples: Siemens Healthineers (too large, but illustrative category), Deutsche Telekom Healthcare, Dräger (equipment — indirect), regional healthcare IT SIs

**South Korea:**
- MFDS regulatory experience (medical device registration)
- Korean-language sales, support, and regulatory team
- Relationships with major Korean hospital groups (Samsung Medical Center, Asan Medical, SNUH supply chain)
- Korean healthcare IT ecosystem experience (OCS/EMR integration)

**Japan (future):**
- PMDA regulatory experience (mandatory)
- Willingness and capability to serve as DMAH (Designated Marketing Authorization Holder)
- Established hospital relationships (university hospital network preferred)
- Full Japanese localization capability
- Examples: Olympus Medical Systems, Terumo, NEC Healthcare, Fujitsu Healthcare solutions

### 4.3 Partner Onboarding Process

1. **RFP / Partner Application**: Formal application with capability evidence
2. **Due Diligence**: Financial assessment, reference checks, site visit
3. **Agreement Negotiation**: Distribution agreement, territory, revenue share, exclusivity terms, performance gates
4. **Certification Training**: 40-hour LumenAI partner certification program (product, regulatory, clinical)
5. **Joint Go-to-Market Planning**: Year 1 plan with named accounts, pipeline milestones, co-marketing
6. **Launch Support**: LumenAI co-sell support for first 3–5 deals
7. **Ongoing Review**: Quarterly business reviews (QBRs); annual partner conference

## 6. Pricing Strategy

### 6.1 Pricing Model Overview

**Base Model:** Per-SPD-department annual SaaS subscription + implementation fee

| Tier | SPD Size | Annual Subscription (USD) | Notes |
|------|----------|--------------------------|-------|
| Starter | < 500 instrument trays/month | $48,000–72,000 | Single department; SME hospital |
| Professional | 500–2,000 trays/month | $84,000–144,000 | Medium hospital; multi-module |
| Enterprise | > 2,000 trays/month | $180,000–360,000+ | Large hospital/health network; custom |
| Health Network | Multi-site (3–10 sites) | Custom ($250K–$1M+) | Enterprise frame; multi-site discount |

### 6.2 Local Currency Invoicing

| Market | Invoice Currency | Base Rate Adjustment | Rationale |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|-----------|
| US | USD | Baseline | Home market |
| Canada | CAD | +3–5% (FX buffer) | Provincial procurement; CAD preferred |
| UK | GBP | +2% | NHS procurement; GBP standard |
| EU (Germany) | EUR | +2% | Public tender; EUR mandatory |
| Australia | AUD | +3% | State health procurement; AUD preferred |
| Singapore | SGD | +2% | GeBIZ; SGD preferred |
| Japan | JPY | +5–8% | Distributor margin; JPY only |
| South Korea | KRW | +5% | Distributor model; KRW |
| UAE | AED | +5–8% | Complex reg/compliance cost built in |
| Saudi Arabia | SAR | +5–10% | Localization + compliance cost premium |

### 6.3 Regional Price Adjustments

- **UK NHS:** NHS framework pricing — may require NHS Supply Chain list price below commercial list; anticipate 10–15% framework discount
- **Australian State Health:** Government framework pricing — anticipate 5–10% government pricing; offset by volume commitment
- **German Public Tender:** Public procurement — price transparency requirement; competitive pricing essential
- **Distributor Margins:** Tier 2/3 markets — distributor receives 25–35% of list price; LumenAI realizes 65–75% of list
