# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this guide is to help LumenAI evaluate customer fit, workflow fit, stakeholder readiness, data boundary feasibility, use case priority, and pilot activation potential.

The guide supports:

- First discovery calls
- Post-demo follow-up calls
- Executive sponsor discussions
- Sterile processing leadership discussions
- Perioperative leadership discussions
- Quality and CAPA stakeholder discussions
- Vendor governance discussions
- Analytics and BI discussions
- Pilot qualification
- Commercial pilot proposal preparation

---

# 2. Discovery Call Objectives

A successful discovery call should answer:

1. What governance problem does the customer care about most?
2. Who owns the problem today?
3. How are SPD quality, CAPA, vendor, and analytics workflows managed now?
4. What reporting gaps or leadership visibility gaps exist?
5. Is a non-PHI pilot feasible?
6. Which stakeholders must participate?
7. Which use case should be prioritized first?
8. What would make a pilot successful?
9. Is there a clear next step toward pilot qualification?

---

# 3. Recommended Call Structure

Recommended call length:
30 to 45 minutes

| Time | Section |
|---:|---|
| 0-5 min | Introductions and purpose |
| 5-10 min | Current-state workflow |
| 10-18 min | Pain points and leadership visibility gaps |
| 18-25 min | Use case fit: CAPA, vendor, Power BI, executive review |
| 25-32 min | Data boundary and pilot feasibility |
| 32-38 min | Stakeholders and decision process |
| 38-45 min | Success criteria and next step |

---

# 4. Opening Script

Thank you for taking the time to speak with me.

The goal today is to understand your current workflow and determine whether LumenAI may be useful in a limited, non-PHI pilot.

I would like to focus on four areas:

- SPD quality visibility
- CAPA recurrence and aging
- Vendor accountability
- Executive reporting and Power BI readiness

The goal is not to force a product fit. The goal is to identify whether there is a real governance problem worth exploring further.

---

# 5. Customer Context Questions

Use these questions to understand the customer environment:

- How many hospitals, surgical sites, or sterile processing locations are in scope?
- Do you have centralized or offsite reprocessing?
- How is sterile processing leadership structured?
- Who owns quality events related to instruments or trays?
- Who owns CAPA follow-up?
- Who owns vendor issue escalation?
- What reporting tools are used today?
- Is Power BI used for operational or executive reporting?
- Are there current transformation, quality, or perioperative improvement initiatives?

---

# 6. Current-State Workflow Questions

## SPD Quality Workflow

- How are instrument, tray, or quality events captured today?
- Are defects tracked consistently across sites?
- How are recurring issues identified?
- How are urgent issues escalated?
- How are quality events summarized for leadership?

## CAPA Workflow

- When does an issue become a CAPA?
- Who assigns CAPAs?
- How are due dates monitored?
- How is CAPA recurrence reviewed?
- How are aging CAPAs escalated?
- What evidence is needed to close a CAPA?

## Vendor Governance Workflow

- How are vendor tray or vendor-related issues captured?
- How are repeat vendor issues identified?
- Are vendor issues linked to CAPA activity?
- Who prepares vendor business reviews?
- How are vendor issues escalated to supply chain or leadership?

## Executive Reporting Workflow

- What reports are reviewed by leadership today?
- How often are SPD quality or CAPA trends reviewed?
- What information is missing from current reports?
- What decisions are difficult to make with current data?
- What would leadership want to see in one summary?

---

# 7. Pain Point Questions

Ask these questions to identify urgency:

- What takes the most time to report manually?
- What issues are repeatedly discussed but hard to quantify?
- What problems are visible to frontline teams but not always visible to executives?
- What gets escalated too late?
- Where do spreadsheets, emails, or manual tracking create risk?
- What quality or vendor issues are hardest to defend with evidence?
- What would improve if leadership had clearer trend visibility?

---

# 8. Use Case Fit Questions

## CAPA Governance Fit

- Do you have recurring CAPA themes?
- Are aging CAPAs a leadership concern?
- Is there a standard escalation threshold?
- Would recurrence and aging visibility be useful?

## Vendor Governance Fit

- Are vendor-related issues recurring?
- Are vendor issues difficult to connect across sites?
- Would vendor score movement or repeat event visibility help?
- Would this support vendor business reviews?

## Power BI / Analytics Fit

- Do you have a BI or analytics team?
- Is Power BI used today?
- What data fields would be useful for executive reporting?
- Would a non-PHI export review be valuable?

## Executive Review Fit

- Would executives benefit from a structured monthly review?
- What should be included in an executive summary?
- Who would need to review pilot findings?
- What would make the executive review useful?

---

# 9. Data Boundary Questions

The first LumenAI pilot can be structured without PHI.

Ask:

- Would a non-PHI pilot be acceptable for initial validation?
- Can demo, synthetic, aggregated, or approved de-identified examples be used?
- What data types are clearly off limits?
- Who would need to approve the data boundary?
- Would IT, privacy, or compliance need to review the pilot?
- Is there a preferred method for sharing sample data?
- Would a CSV export review be easier than system integration?

---

# 10. Stakeholder Mapping Questions

Identify the decision and validation group:

- Who would be the executive sponsor?
- Who would own the pilot operationally?
- Who would represent SPD?
- Who would represent perioperative services?
- Who would represent quality or CAPA?
- Who would represent supply chain or vendor governance?
- Who would represent analytics or BI?
- Who would represent IT security or privacy?
- Who signs off on pilot scope?
- Who decides whether to expand after the pilot?

---

# 11. Pilot Scope Questions

Ask:

- Which site or department would be best for a limited pilot?
- Should the pilot focus on one hospital or multiple sites?
- Which use case should come first?
- What timeframe would be realistic?
- How many stakeholders should participate?
- What meetings or governance reviews already exist?
- What evidence should be captured during the pilot?
- What would be out of scope for the first pilot?

---

# 12. Pilot Success Criteria Questions

Ask:

- What would make the pilot valuable?
- What would leadership need to see?
- What would SPD leaders need to see?
- What would quality leaders need to see?
- What would supply chain leaders need to see?
- What would analytics leaders need to see?
- What would make the pilot worth expanding?
- What would make the pilot not worth pursuing?

---

# 13. Commercial Readiness Questions

Ask carefully after problem fit is established:

- Is there budget for pilot innovation or operational improvement?
- Is there a standard process for evaluating pilot tools?
- Would procurement need to be involved?
- Who owns the commercial decision?
- Is this more likely to start as a paid pilot, no-cost evaluation, or strategic review?
- What timeline would make sense for a next decision?
- What barriers could slow approval?

# 16. Red Flags

Red flags include:

- No clear problem owner
- No executive sponsor potential
- No willingness to discuss data boundary
- No stakeholder availability
- No clear use case fit
- No reporting pain
- No interest in follow-up
- No pilot decision pathway

---

# 17. Green Flags

Green flags include:

- Known SPD quality or vendor reporting pain
- Recurring CAPA concerns
- Manual spreadsheet reporting burden
- Multi-site governance complexity
- Power BI or analytics interest
- Executive sponsor curiosity
- Non-PHI pilot openness
- Clear next-step owner
- Willingness to schedule follow-up

---

# 18. Discovery Close Script

Based on what we discussed, it sounds like the strongest potential use case may be:

- [CAPA governance]
- [Vendor governance]
- [Power BI reporting]
- [Executive review]
- [Other]

The next best step would be to confirm stakeholders, data boundary, pilot scope, and success criteria.

Would it make sense to schedule a follow-up pilot qualification session with the right operational, quality, analytics, and executive stakeholders?
