## Final Determination
The LumenAI v1.5 Pilot Qualification Scorecard v1 is drafted.

This scorecard provides a structured way to determine whether a prospect is ready for a LumenAI pilot based on problem urgency, stakeholder readiness, data boundary feasibility, workflow fit, use case fit, and commercial pathway clarity.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this scorecard is to help LumenAI qualify prospective customers after outreach, demo, or discovery conversations.

The scorecard supports:

- Pilot readiness assessment
- Customer fit evaluation
- Stakeholder readiness review
- Data boundary feasibility review
- Use case prioritization
- Commercial pathway assessment
- Follow-up recommendation
- Proposal readiness decision-making

---

# 2. When to Use This Scorecard

Use this scorecard after:

- Initial outreach response
- Discovery call
- Demo call
- Executive sponsor conversation
- Stakeholder mapping session
- Pilot scoping conversation
- Strategic partner referral

Do not use this scorecard before there is enough information to evaluate the customer.

---

# 3. Scoring Model

Each category is scored from 1 to 5.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Strong fit / clear evidence / immediate pilot potential |
| 4 | Good fit / some evidence / near-term pilot potential |
| 3 | Moderate fit / needs more discovery |
| 2 | Weak fit / significant gaps |
| 1 | Not a fit / no evidence / no readiness |

Maximum score:
50 points

---

# 4. Qualification Categories

## Category 1 — Problem Urgency

Question:
Does the customer have a real and urgent governance visibility problem?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Clear pain around SPD quality, CAPA recurrence, vendor accountability, or executive reporting |
| 4 | Strong pain exists but is not fully quantified |
| 3 | Some pain exists but urgency is unclear |
| 2 | Pain is weak or not tied to leadership priorities |
| 1 | No clear problem |

Evidence to look for:
- Manual reporting burden
- Recurring quality issues
- CAPA aging concerns
- Vendor trend concerns
- Executive visibility gaps
- Multi-site reporting complexity

---

## Category 2 — Executive Sponsor Potential

Question:
Is there a likely executive or senior leader who would sponsor or support a pilot?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Executive sponsor identified and interested |
| 4 | Likely sponsor identified but not yet engaged |
| 3 | Sponsor role is possible but unclear |
| 2 | Functional interest exists but no executive path |
| 1 | No sponsor potential |

Evidence to look for:
- COO, VP, CQO, perioperative executive, or hospital president interest
- Senior leader asks for follow-up
- Leader connects LumenAI to a known priority
- Decision owner can be identified

---

## Category 3 — Workflow Fit

Question:
Does LumenAI fit the customer’s current workflow or governance needs?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Strong fit across SPD, quality, vendor, and executive review workflows |
| 4 | Good fit in at least two workflows |
| 3 | Fit exists but needs more mapping |
| 2 | Workflow fit is limited or unclear |
| 1 | No workflow fit |

Evidence to look for:
- Existing governance meetings
- Existing manual tracking
- Need for executive-ready summaries
- CAPA or vendor review process
- Multi-site operational review

---

## Category 4 — Non-PHI Pilot Feasibility

Question:
Can the customer evaluate LumenAI without live PHI or heavy system integration?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Non-PHI pilot clearly feasible |
| 4 | Non-PHI pilot likely feasible with approval |
| 3 | Feasible but requires IT/privacy clarification |
| 2 | Significant uncertainty or approval barriers |
| 1 | Non-PHI pilot not feasible |

Evidence to look for:
- Acceptance of demo, synthetic, aggregated, or de-identified examples
- Willingness to review CSV/export model
- Low-burden pilot interest
- IT/privacy review pathway identified

---

Question:
Is CAPA recurrence, aging, escalation, or evidence tracking a relevant use case?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | CAPA use case is highly relevant and validated |
| 4 | CAPA use case is relevant but needs examples |
| 3 | CAPA use case may be relevant |
| 2 | CAPA use case is weak |
| 1 | CAPA use case not relevant |

Evidence to look for:
- CAPA aging concerns
- Repeat issue concerns
- Manual CAPA tracking
- Need for escalation visibility
- Quality leader interest

## Category 6 — Vendor Governance Fit

Question:
Is vendor accountability, repeat vendor event tracking, or vendor business review support relevant?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Vendor governance use case is highly relevant and validated |
| 4 | Vendor use case is relevant but needs more workflow detail |
| 3 | Vendor use case may be relevant |

| 1 | Vendor use case not relevant |

Evidence to look for:
- Repeat vendor tray issues
- Vendor escalation challenges

- Business review preparation needs
- CAPA-linked vendor events

## Category 7 — Power BI / Analytics Fit

Question:
Would Power BI, export structure, data dictionary, or executive reporting validation be useful?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Strong Power BI or analytics fit |
| 4 | Analytics interest exists and BI stakeholder can be engaged |
| 3 | Reporting need exists but BI path is unclear |
| 2 | Limited analytics fit |
| 1 | No analytics or reporting fit |

Evidence to look for:
- Power BI used today
- Manual Excel reporting
- Dashboard gaps
- BI team interest
- Need for executive KPIs

---

## Category 8 — Stakeholder Readiness

Question:
Are the right stakeholders available or identifiable?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Executive, operational, quality, vendor, and analytics stakeholders identified |
| 4 | Most key stakeholders identified |
| 3 | Some stakeholders identified, more mapping needed |
| 2 | Only one stakeholder engaged |
| 1 | No stakeholder readiness |

Evidence to look for:
- Named pilot owner
- Named executive sponsor
- SPD leader involved
- Quality leader involved
- Analytics or IT stakeholder identified
- Follow-up participants proposed

---

## Category 9 — Commercial Pathway Clarity

Question:
Is there a path from pilot to commercial decision?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Commercial decision owner, budget path, and procurement path are clear |
| 4 | Commercial path likely but not fully confirmed |
| 3 | Commercial path possible but unclear |
| 2 | Interest exists but no commercial pathway |
| 1 | No commercial pathway |

Evidence to look for:
- Budget owner identified
- Paid pilot possible
- Procurement process understood
- Sponsor asks about pricing or proposal
- Expansion path discussed

---

## Category 10 — Next-Step Commitment

Question:
Did the customer commit to a meaningful next step?

Score guidance:

| Score | Description |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Customer commits to pilot qualification or proposal discussion |
| 4 | Customer commits to discovery follow-up with stakeholders |
| 3 | Customer requests information or internal review |
| 2 | Customer gives vague interest |
| 1 | No next step |

Evidence to look for:
- Follow-up call scheduled
- Stakeholders invited
- Data boundary review requested
- Demo requested
- Proposal requested
- Referral made

# 6. Qualification Interpretation

| Total Score | Interpretation | Recommended Action |
|---:|---|---|
| 42-50 | Strong pilot candidate | Move to pilot proposal or pilot scoping |
| 34-41 | Good pilot candidate | Schedule qualification follow-up |
| 26-33 | Continue discovery | Clarify stakeholders, use case, and data boundary |
| 18-25 | Nurture | Send resources and revisit later |
| Below 18 | Not ready | Do not prioritize for pilot activation |

---

# 7. Pilot Pathway Recommendation

## Pathway A — Move to Pilot Proposal

Use when:
- Score is 42 or higher
- Executive sponsor is identified
- Non-PHI pilot is feasible
- At least one use case is strongly validated
- Next step is clear

Recommended action:
Prepare commercial pilot proposal.

---

## Pathway B — Schedule Qualification Follow-Up

Use when:
- Score is 34 to 41
- Problem fit is strong
- More stakeholder, data boundary, or commercial detail is needed

Recommended action:
Schedule a pilot qualification session.

---

## Pathway C — Continue Discovery

Use when:
- Score is 26 to 33
- Interest exists but use case, stakeholder, or pilot fit is unclear

Recommended action:
Continue discovery and refine use case.

---

## Pathway D — Nurture

Use when:
- Score is 18 to 25
- Customer is interested but not ready

Recommended action:
Send resources and revisit later.

---

## Pathway E — Not Ready

Use when:
- Score is below 18
- No clear problem, stakeholder, or next step exists

Recommended action:
Do not prioritize for pilot activation.

---

# 8. Minimum Pilot Activation Requirements

Before moving to pilot proposal, confirm:

- Problem urgency is 4 or higher
- Executive sponsor potential is 3 or higher
- Non-PHI pilot feasibility is 3 or higher
- At least one use case category is 4 or higher
- Next-step commitment is 4 or higher
- Total score is 34 or higher

---

# 9. Red Flag Rules

Do not move to pilot proposal if:

- No sponsor can be identified
- Customer will not discuss data boundary
- No use case resonates
- No operational owner exists
- No follow-up owner exists
- Customer wants production integration before validating value
- Customer cannot define success criteria

---

# 10. Green Flag Rules

Strongly consider pilot proposal if:

- Customer has multi-site SPD complexity
- Executive sponsor shows interest
- CAPA or vendor issue visibility is a clear pain point
- Manual reporting burden is high
- Customer uses Power BI or Excel reporting
- Non-PHI pilot is accepted
- Multiple stakeholders agree to discovery
- Customer asks about scope, timeline, or proposal
